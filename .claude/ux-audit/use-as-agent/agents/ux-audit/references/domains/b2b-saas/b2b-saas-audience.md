## Competitor-Switcher (Sub-state of Product-Aware)

The visitor already uses a rival product and is actively evaluating whether to switch. They have direct experience with a competitor's limitations.

**What they need:** Not "here's the problem" or "here's the category" but "here's what you're giving up by staying with [Competitor]." Acknowledge their current state; frame switching as a rational upgrade, not a risky leap.

**Content signals:**
- Comparison/vs. pages (Pattern I) are the primary conversion vehicle
- Hero format: "The [Category] for teams who've outgrown [Competitor]"
- Social proof: explicit switcher testimonials
- Migration-specific content reduces primary anxiety
- Pattern I must include genuine competitor acknowledgment — concede strengths, differentiate on weaknesses

## Existing Customer (Upsell/Cross-Sell)

Not acquisition — expansion. The visitor is already "most-aware" by definition.

**Relevant framework — Expansion Readiness:**
1. **Usage threshold** — Has the customer hit limits making upgrade functionally necessary?
2. **Value realization** — Has the customer achieved enough value to trust upgrading will deliver more?
3. **Organizational expansion** — Are additional teams or use cases emerging?

**Unique anxieties:** "Will upgrading break my current setup?" / "Am I paying for features I won't use?" / "Will my team need retraining?"

**Trust signals:** In-product usage data ("You've saved 47 hours this quarter — Pro users save 3x more"), peer benchmarking ("Teams your size typically use Pro"), risk-free tier trial.

## Enterprise Evaluation Committee

Multiple stakeholders (6–10 per Gartner, up to 13 per Forrester), each with different concerns evaluating the same product simultaneously. No single awareness level applies.

**Page strategy — modular sections:**
- **Executive:** ROI, business case, strategic value → ROI calculator, analyst reports
- **Technical:** Architecture, security, integrations, API docs → security whitepaper, technical docs
- **Procurement:** Pricing structure, SLA terms, compliance certifications → DPA, procurement contact
- **End-user:** Product demo, feature walkthrough, ease-of-use → interactive demo, trial signup

**Navigation implication:** Enterprise landing pages should include anchor links or tab interface for each stakeholder concern. The buying committee consults 4–5 pieces of content per member before group discussions.
