# Government-Institutional: Archetypes

Domain knowledge for government and institutional site archetypes — primary structural models.
Part of: references/domains/government-institutional/

---

## Archetype 1: Service Delivery Portal

**Optimizes for:** task completion rate for civic services (apply for permit, pay tax, renew license).

**Best fit:** government service portals (gov.uk, gov.pl), municipal websites, public utility sites.

**Sacrifices:** brand personality, visual flair — clarity and accessibility trump aesthetics.

**Structure:**
1. Task-first homepage ("What do you need to do?")
2. Service catalog organized by life events and task types
3. Guided forms with step-by-step progression
4. Application status tracking

**Key characteristics:**
- Every page answers "what can I do here?" within 3 seconds
- Navigation mirrors citizen mental models (life events), not departmental org charts
- Forms are the product — form UX is the entire conversion funnel
- Authentication tied to national/regional digital ID systems
- Mobile-first is non-negotiable — many citizens access only via phone

**Reference implementations:** GOV.UK, gov.pl, Denmark Borger.dk, Singapore GovTech

## Archetype 2: Informational Institution

**Optimizes for:** information findability and public trust.

**Best fit:** universities, hospitals, museums, libraries, public agencies.

**Sacrifices:** content brevity — depth and completeness are required even when they create long pages.

**Multi-audience challenge:** serves radically different audiences simultaneously.
- University example: prospective students AND current students AND faculty AND parents AND alumni
- Hospital example: patients AND referring physicians AND researchers AND job seekers

**Structure:**
1. Audience-routed homepage ("I am a...")
2. Deep content hierarchies per audience segment
3. Search-heavy navigation (users often bypass menus)
4. News/events as trust and relevance signals
5. Emergency/alert information always accessible

**Key characteristics:**
- Homepage is a routing mechanism, not a content page
- Search is primary navigation for repeat visitors
- Content governance is the #1 challenge — hundreds of content owners across departments
- Visual identity serves trust and recognition, not conversion
- Accessibility compliance extends to all uploaded documents (PDFs, etc.)

## Archetype 3: Regulatory/Compliance Site

**Optimizes for:** legal compliance and public record access.

**Best fit:** regulatory agencies, courts, compliance databases, legislative archives.

**Sacrifices:** user experience — content completeness and legal accuracy are non-negotiable even when they hurt usability.

**Structure:**
1. Searchable regulation/legislation database
2. Filing/submission portals for regulated entities
3. Public records and decision archives
4. Compliance guides and interpretation documents
5. Contact/inquiry system for compliance questions

**Key characteristics:**
- Legal text cannot be simplified beyond a threshold without losing legal meaning
- Version control of regulations is critical — users must see which version was in effect on a specific date
- Two-tier content: plain-language summaries for general public + full legal text for professionals
- Audit trails and transparency requirements affect every interaction
- Download/print functionality is essential — legal documents are often needed offline
- Search must support exact legal citation lookup alongside natural language queries
