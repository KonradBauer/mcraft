## Local Business Discovery Guidance

### Key Assumptions to Form
- **Primary conversion action**: Phone calls / online bookings / walk-ins / online orders?
- **Location model**: Single fixed location / service area / multi-location?
- **Mobile traffic percentage**: Assume 65%+ unless told otherwise
- **Google reviews**: Rating and count? (Strong = feature prominently; weak = focus on other trust signals)
- **Service count**: 1-3 core services (simple nav) / 5+ services (needs service pages)?
- **Appointment-based**: Yes (needs booking system) / no (phone/walk-in)?
- **Emergency services**: 24/7 or after-hours availability?

### Common Project Shapes
- **Service provider (plumber, lawyer)**: Proximity-Action archetype, phone-first, service area focus
- **Appointment business (salon, dentist)**: Booking-First archetype, embedded scheduling
- **Restaurant**: Menu-First archetype, food photography, online ordering
- **Multi-location chain**: Multi-Location archetype, location finder, brand consistency

### Unique Constraints
- **NAP consistency**: Name, Address, Phone must match Google My Business exactly (SEO critical)
- **Mobile dominance**: 65%+ mobile traffic — design mobile-first without exception
- **Google My Business**: Often more visited than the website — site must complement, not compete
- **Local SEO**: Service + location keywords in H1s, title tags, and meta descriptions
- **Schema markup**: LocalBusiness structured data for rich snippets in search
- **PDF menus**: Never — must be HTML for indexability, accessibility, and mobile usability

### Default Assumptions
If no info provided: Single-location local service business, 3-5 core services, 65% mobile traffic, Google Maps listing exists, 4.0+ Google rating, primary conversion = phone call, serves within 15-mile radius.
