## Portfolio Discovery Guidance

### Key Assumptions to Form
- **Practitioner type**: Solo freelancer / creative partner / small studio?
- **Primary service**: Design / development / strategy / writing / photography?
- **Target client**: Industry, company size, budget level
- **Traffic source**: Referrals (site validates trust) / cold traffic (site must build trust)?
- **Specialization**: One niche or multiple services?
- **Project value**: $500 projects need different UX than $50K projects
- **Pricing transparency**: Prices on site? Starting rates? Nothing?

### Common Project Shapes
- **Solo freelancer (strategy)**: Single-page, Credibility-Led archetype, 3-5 case studies
- **Solo creative (design/photo)**: Gallery-style, Showcase-Led archetype, visual-first
- **Author/speaker**: Multi-page, Thought-Leader archetype, content-heavy
- **Small studio**: Multi-page, Agency-Hybrid archetype, team + case studies

### Unique Constraints
- **Curation over volume**: Fewer projects = stronger impression. 3-5 focused projects beat 20 scattered ones
- **First-person voice**: Corporate third-person destroys the personal connection
- **Real photography**: Stock photos immediately undermine credibility for someone selling creative services
- **CTA specificity**: "Book a 20-min chat" outperforms "Contact Us" — reduce uncertainty about what happens next
- **Mobile considerations**: Less critical than e-commerce (most traffic is desktop from LinkedIn/email), but still needs to work

### Default Assumptions
If no info provided: Solo freelancer, 1-3 core services, project-based work ($3K-15K range), traffic primarily from referrals and LinkedIn, 3-6 portfolio projects to showcase, prefers inquiry form over booking link.

### Branding Agency / Creative Studio

Use these questions when the brief mentions an agency, studio, branding firm, creative consultancy, or any organization whose primary service is brand work for clients.

**Q1: Solo practitioner or agency/studio?**
"Is this site for you personally, or for a team/agency you run?"
→ Solo → routes to Credibility-Led or Showcase-Led archetype
→ Agency / studio → routes to Agency-Hybrid or Agency-Studio-Led archetype
→ Agency with enterprise clients (CMO buyers, multi-month engagements) → Agency-Studio-Led

**Q2: Who is the primary buyer?**
"Who typically hires you — a CMO, a CEO, a founder, a design director, a procurement team?"
→ CMO / Marketing Director → Agency-Studio-Led archetype, case study framing emphasizes strategic rationale and business outcomes
→ Founder / CEO → Credibility-Led framing, outcome metrics more prominent
→ Design Director → Showcase-Led quality signals, Awwwards-level craft emphasis
→ Procurement / RFP → Process page critical, credentials and methodology documentation

**Q3: Sector focus or sector-agnostic?**
"Do you work in specific industries, or are you deliberately sector-agnostic?"
→ Sector-specific (e.g., healthcare, fintech, consumer goods) → filter bar organized around deep sector expertise; case studies front-load sector-matched work
→ Sector-agnostic → filter bar organized around service type; lead with range and creative voice

**Q4: How many active case studies?**
"How many completed projects can you feature — and for how many do you have client permission to publish?"
→ 6–12 publishable projects → curated portfolio (Agency-Studio-Led default)
→ 13–30 projects → consider curated 12 + full archive page
→ 3–5 projects → does not support grid; consider full-case-study homepage (one featured project)
→ < 3 publishable → explore whether process work, speculative work, or internal projects can supplement

**Q5: Quantified business impact data?**
"For past projects, do you have measurable business outcomes — sales lift, awareness metrics, market share? Or is your evidence primarily qualitative and visual?"
→ Has metrics → metrics-led case study structure; business outcome before creative reveal
→ Visual quality only → Variant A narrative structure (see Case Study Structure); leads with strategic rationale, not metrics

**Q6: Motion / kinetic identity in offering?**
"Is motion design — title sequences, UI animation, kinetic brand systems — part of what you sell?"
→ Yes → include autoplay motion in case study heroes; consider Process Variant D (tool documentation); add "Motion Identity" as service tag in filter bar
→ No → static imagery preferred; no motion in case study hero (avoids implying motion capability)
