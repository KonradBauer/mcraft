# Nonprofit: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for nonprofit / cause organization sites.
Part of: references/domains/nonprofit/

---

Archetypes define what a site optimizes for at the architectural level — how pages connect, what narrative the site tells, and which user journey is prioritized.

**These are SITE-LEVEL archetypes** (what the overall site structure optimizes for). They are distinct from PAGE-LEVEL layout patterns in `patterns.md`, which define the internal structure of individual pages.

---

## Empathy-Action (Cause-Driven)
**Optimizes for:** Donation conversion rate + average gift size + recurring donor rate.
**Best fit:** Charities, NGOs, advocacy organizations, foundations, community organizations, humanitarian causes — any organization funded primarily by donations and volunteer labor.
**Sacrifices:** Product education, feature comparison, service descriptions. The site optimizes for emotional connection and trust in impact, not rational evaluation.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with mission statement + emotional hero image/video + prominent "Donate" button in contrasting color, persistent in nav
- Navigation: Mission/purpose pages first, "Donate" always visible in nav as high-contrast CTA, "Get Involved" as secondary path
- Key pages: Homepage, About/Mission, Programs/Impact, Donate, Volunteer/Get Involved, Stories/News, Contact
- Social proof: Impact metrics ("12,000 meals served"), beneficiary stories, donor count, charity rating badges (GuideStar, Charity Navigator), media logos
- Donation form sophistication: recurring-first default, gift array with impact-per-amount framing, express pay above card fields, impact calculator or "choose your impact" tool
- Storytelling architecture: identifiable-victim hero modules, emotional pacing (intensity > legibility > agency), scrollytelling campaign landings with embedded donate

## Advocacy-First (Movement-Driven)
**Optimizes for:** Petition signatures, letter-writing campaigns, social shares, and grassroots mobilization — political/social action over financial donations.
**Best fit:** Advocacy organizations, political action committees, social justice movements, environmental campaigns, policy reform organizations.
**Sacrifices:** Institutional trust-building. The site prioritizes urgency and collective action over organizational credibility.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with urgent issue framing + "Take Action" CTA (sign petition, contact representative, share)
- Navigation: Take Action, Issues, Stories, About, Donate (secondary)
- Key pages: Issue pages (one per campaign), Take Action (with tools), Stories/Impact, About, Donate
- Social proof: Petition signature count, supporter count, media coverage, endorsements, social share counts
- Petition-to-donate conversion path: micro-commitment ladder (sign > share > donate), post-action recurring upgrade prompt, foot-in-the-door sequencing
- Campaign storytelling: urgency-driven narrative arcs per issue, community-scale framing over individual-victim (collectivist appeal), tease > commit > resolve cross-page pattern

## Service-Provider (Community Org)
**Optimizes for:** Service access for beneficiaries — helping people find and use the organization's programs.
**Best fit:** Community centers, food banks, shelters, health clinics, legal aid, social services — organizations where the primary audience is people seeking help, not donors.
**Sacrifices:** Fundraising optimization. Donor-facing content exists but doesn't dominate. The site serves the served.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with "Need Help?" pathway + clear service directory
- Navigation: Services/Programs, Eligibility, Locations, Contact, About, Donate (utility nav)
- Key pages: Service directory, individual Program pages with eligibility, Locations/Hours, Contact, FAQs, Donate
- Social proof: Impact statistics, beneficiary testimonials, community partner logos
- Beneficiary consent and dignity: storytelling uses dignity-preserving framing (agency over victimhood), emotional pacing alternates need with hope/progress
- Service access storytelling: "Need Help?" pathway uses orientation > legibility > agency sequence, clear information scent from service teaser to eligibility detail

## Foundation / Grant-Maker
**Optimizes for:** Grant application quality and institutional credibility — attracting qualified applicants and reporting to stakeholders.
**Best fit:** Private foundations, corporate foundations, community foundations — organizations that distribute funds rather than raise them.
**Sacrifices:** Emotional storytelling. The tone is institutional, professional, and data-driven rather than emotionally compelling.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with mission + current funding priorities + grant deadlines
- Navigation: Grant Programs, Apply, Grantees/Impact, About, News
- Key pages: Grant programs (with eligibility), Application portal, Grantee directory, Annual report, About/Board
- Social proof: Grant amounts distributed, grantee count, impact reports, board member credentials
- Impact reporting depth: annual report with data visualization (progress bars, outcome charts), grantee outcome dashboards, cost-to-outcome transparency modules
- Data storytelling: IPOP-style pages (Ideas + People + Evidence + Interactive) for stakeholder reporting, quantified community-scale narrative over individual stories

## Archetype Selection

When choosing an archetype:

1. **Primary audience** — Donors? Beneficiaries? Advocates? Grant applicants?
2. **Primary conversion** — Donate? Volunteer? Sign petition? Apply for services?
3. **Funding model** — Individual donations? Grants? Events? Government funding?

**Default combinations:**
- Traditional charity: Empathy-Action
- Advocacy/political org: Advocacy-First
- Food bank, shelter, community center: Service-Provider
- Private/corporate foundation: Foundation / Grant-Maker
