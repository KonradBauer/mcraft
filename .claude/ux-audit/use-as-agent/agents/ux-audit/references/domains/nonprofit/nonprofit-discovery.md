## Nonprofit Discovery Guidance

### Key Assumptions to Form
- **Primary funding model**: Individual donations / grants / events / government / corporate sponsorship?
- **Primary audience**: Individual donors / corporate sponsors / volunteers / beneficiaries?
- **Cause area**: Humanitarian / environmental / health / education / advocacy?
- **Impact data quality**: Strong metrics (feature prominently) / weak metrics (lead with stories)?
- **Online donation platform**: Existing? Which platform? None?
- **Geographic scope**: Local community / national / international?
- **Tax status**: 501(c)(3) (tax-deductible) / 501(c)(4) (advocacy/lobbying)?

- **Donation Model & Infrastructure**
  - Which donation platform? (native form, Stripe, PayU, external redirect)
  - Recurring donation support? (monthly, quarterly, annual)
  - Mobile donation share estimate (affects form design priority)
  - Express payment methods available? (Apple Pay, Google Pay, BLIK)
  - Average gift range (affects suggested amount array design)
- **Storytelling Readiness & Beneficiary Consent**
  - Does the org have beneficiary stories ready to publish?
  - Identifiable beneficiaries or anonymized only?
  - Photo/video consent process in place? (especially for children)
  - RODO/GDPR compliance for beneficiary imagery?
  - Story update cadence (one-time or ongoing narrative)
- **Emotional Design & Narrative Pacing**
  - Primary motivation type of target donors (empathy, efficacy, social, mixed)
  - Belief barriers to address (awareness, concern, efficacy, trust)
  - Compassion fatigue risk (cause area sensitivity level)
  - Tone preference (urgent/emotional vs. hopeful/empowering)

### Common Project Shapes
- **Traditional charity**: Empathy-Action archetype, donation-focused, story-forward
- **Advocacy org**: Advocacy-First archetype, petition/action-focused, urgency-driven
- **Community center / food bank**: Service-Provider archetype, beneficiary-focused, accessibility-first
- **Foundation**: Foundation archetype, grant-focused, institutional tone

### Unique Constraints
- **Emotional imagery ethics**: Real beneficiary photos (with consent), never exploitative. Dignity over pity.
- **Multi-audience balancing**: Donors, volunteers, beneficiaries, media all use the same site with different needs
- **Tax-deductibility**: Must communicate clearly if donations are tax-deductible
- **Payment processing**: Donation platforms (Stripe, PayPal Giving Fund, Network for Good) have different fee structures
- **Recurring giving**: Monthly donors have 5x higher lifetime value — always offer the option
- **Year-end urgency**: November-December generates 30%+ of annual donations — site must support seasonal campaigns
- **Accessibility**: Beneficiary audiences may have lower digital literacy — plain language, simple navigation

### Default Assumptions
If no info provided: 501(c)(3) charity, primary funding from individual donations, serves donors + volunteers + beneficiaries, Charity Navigator rated, no existing online donation platform, 50/50 desktop/mobile traffic, most traffic from social media and email campaigns.
