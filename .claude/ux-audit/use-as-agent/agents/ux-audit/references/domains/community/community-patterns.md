# Community: Page Layout Patterns

Domain knowledge for community and membership sites.
Part of: references/domains/community/

---

Select a pattern based on the community type, membership model, and belonging readiness level of the target audience.

**Narrative arc: Identity → Proof → Access** — All community page layouts follow this three-phase structure:
- **Identity**: Hook the visitor by naming who this community is for and what they do together
- **Proof**: Show the community is alive and valuable through activity metrics, previews, and testimonials
- **Access**: Close with a clear membership path that feels like joining, not purchasing

---

## A. Community Landing Page
**Use when:** Recruiting members to a gated community with free or paid tiers.
**Core narrative:** "This is where [identity group] comes to [shared activity] — and you belong here."
**Structure:**
1. **Belonging Hero**: Community name, identity statement ("For [identity group] who [shared activity]"), member count, primary CTA, member avatar collage (5-8 real photos in overlapping row).
2. **Sample Content Preview**: 2-3 curated posts from the community, blurred or truncated to show quality without giving away full value. Each shows author avatar, timestamp, excerpt, engagement metrics.
3. **What Members Get**: Structured benefit list — forums/discussions, live events, content library, member directory, creator/expert access. Use icons + short descriptions, not paragraphs.
4. **Activity Proof**: "This week in [Community]: 47 new discussions, 3 live events, 12 new members." Real-time or recent metrics that prove the community is alive.
5. **Member Testimonials**: Quotes about belonging and outcomes, not feature praise. "I found my first three clients here" > "Great platform."
6. **Creator/Founder Section**: Photo, bio, credentials, "why I built this" statement. Essential for creator-led communities; optional for peer-led.
7. **Membership Tiers and Pricing**: Free vs paid differentiation, feature comparison, annual discount option, "Cancel anytime" reassurance.
8. **FAQ and Final CTA**: Address top objections (Is it active? Can I cancel? What platform is it on?) + repeated join CTA.

---

## B. Membership Pricing Page
**Use when:** Community has paid tiers and needs a dedicated pricing comparison.
**Core narrative:** "Choose the level of access that fits your needs."
**Structure:**
1. **Tier Comparison**: Side-by-side cards for free vs paid tiers. Highlight the recommended tier visually (border, badge, scale).
2. **Feature Matrix**: Detailed breakdown of what each tier includes — content access, event access, directory, direct messaging, creator access.
3. **Member Testimonials Per Tier**: Segment social proof by tier — free members praising value even at free level, paid members validating the upgrade.
4. **FAQ About Billing/Cancellation**: Payment methods, billing cycle, cancellation process, refund policy, "what happens to my content if I cancel."
5. **CTA with Reassurance**: "Start free" or "Join [Tier Name]" + "Cancel anytime" + "No credit card required" (if applicable).

---

## C. Community About / Mission Page
**Use when:** Community needs to establish purpose and credibility beyond the landing page.
**Core narrative:** "Here's why this community exists and who's behind it."
**Structure:**
1. **Mission Statement**: One clear sentence about why this community exists and who it serves. Not corporate — personal and specific.
2. **Founder Story**: The origin narrative — what problem did the founder experience, what gap did they see, what prompted building this community.
3. **Community Values**: 3-5 principles that define the community culture. These signal to prospects whether they'll fit in.
4. **Community Stats/Milestones**: Member count, countries represented, discussions held, events run, years active. Timeline format for established communities.
5. **Join CTA**: Final invitation with belonging language — "Ready to join us?" not "Sign up now."
