# E-commerce: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for e-commerce / DTC sites.
Part of: references/domains/ecommerce/

---

Archetypes define what a site optimizes for at the architectural level — how pages connect, what narrative the site tells, and which user journey is prioritized.

**These are SITE-LEVEL archetypes** (what the overall site structure optimizes for). They are distinct from PAGE-LEVEL layout patterns in `patterns.md`, which define the internal structure of individual pages.

---

## Transaction-First (DTC Storefront)
**Optimizes for:** Revenue per session (add-to-cart rate × checkout completion rate).
**Best fit:** DTC product brands, Shopify stores, fashion/apparel, beauty, food/beverage, home goods — any business selling physical products directly to consumers.
**Sacrifices:** Long-form education, lead nurturing, relationship-building content. The site assumes desire already exists or can be created in one session.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with flagship product or seasonal campaign hero, trust bar beneath header ("Free shipping · 30-day returns · 50,000+ happy customers")
- Navigation: Category-driven mega-menu with quick-access to "New," "Best Sellers," "Sale" — not capability-based nav
- Key pages: Homepage, Collection/Category pages, Product Detail Page (PDP), Cart, Checkout, About/Brand Story, FAQ/Shipping & Returns
- Social proof: Customer reviews with photos/UGC, star ratings on product cards, real-time purchase notifications, "As seen in" press logos

---

## Catalog-Led (Large Retailer)
**Optimizes for:** Product findability and comparison across a large inventory (500+ SKUs).
**Best fit:** Multi-brand retailers, department stores, electronics, home improvement — businesses with deep catalogs where search and filtering are primary navigation.
**Sacrifices:** Brand storytelling, emotional connection. The site is a utility — visitors come to find and compare, not to be inspired.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with category navigation grid or seasonal promotions, search bar prominent
- Navigation: Deep category tree with mega-menu, faceted filters on category pages
- Key pages: Homepage, Category pages with filters, PDP with specifications, Comparison tool, Cart, Checkout
- Social proof: Review aggregates, "Best Seller" badges, "X people viewing this" indicators

---

## Brand-Story (Premium DTC)
**Optimizes for:** Brand perception and emotional connection that justifies premium pricing.
**Best fit:** Premium/luxury DTC brands, artisan products, sustainable brands, brands where the story IS the differentiator — any product where perceived value matters more than feature comparison.
**Sacrifices:** Conversion speed, price transparency, volume. The site deliberately slows the visitor down to build desire and justify price.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with cinematic hero (lifestyle imagery or video), editorial-style layout
- Navigation: Minimal, curated — "Our Story," "Shop," "Journal" — not category-heavy
- Key pages: Homepage, Brand Story, Shop (curated, not category-driven), Journal/Blog, About, FAQ
- Social proof: Press features ("As seen in Vogue"), influencer partnerships, founder story, craftsmanship details

---

## Subscription-First
**Optimizes for:** Subscriber acquisition and retention (trial-to-paid conversion × churn reduction).
**Best fit:** Subscription boxes, replenishment products (vitamins, coffee, pet food), meal kits, beauty boxes — any DTC product with a recurring purchase model.
**Sacrifices:** One-time purchase simplicity. The site channels visitors toward the subscription, sometimes making one-time purchases deliberately harder to find.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with subscription value prop ("Your personalized box, delivered monthly"), quiz or customization CTA
- Navigation: "How It Works," "Plans," "What's Inside," "Reviews" — subscription-journey nav
- Key pages: Homepage, How It Works (3-step explainer), Plans/Pricing, Quiz/Customization, Reviews, Gift
- Social proof: Unboxing UGC, subscriber count ("500K+ subscribers"), retention metrics ("92% stay after 3 months")

---

## Archetype Selection

When choosing an archetype:

1. **Catalog size** — How many products? (<50 = curated DTC; 500+ = catalog-led)
2. **Price point** — Premium ($100+) favors brand-story; mass-market favors transaction-first
3. **Purchase frequency** — Recurring = subscription-first; one-time considered = brand-story or transaction-first
4. **Brand vs. commodity** — Strong brand identity = brand-story; commodity products = transaction-first or catalog-led

**Default combinations:**
- Most Shopify DTC brands: Transaction-First
- Large multi-brand retailers: Catalog-Led
- Premium/artisan DTC: Brand-Story
- Subscription boxes/replenishment: Subscription-First
