# B2B: Audience Psychology

Domain knowledge shared across all B2B site types.
Part of: references/domains/b2b/

## Awareness Model

The B2B awareness model determines messaging strategy and page structure. Each level maps to a content approach.

| Level | Visitor knows... | Opening strategy | Primary content |
|-------|------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Category-Unaware** | Experiences symptoms but has no framework for the solution | Symptom headline — describe the pain, then name the category | Educational content; JTBD framing; blog/content marketing as primary channel |
| **Problem-Aware** | Has a problem but doesn't know solutions exist | Problem validation + category introduction | "How it works" as category education; comparison with status quo |
| **Solution-Aware** | Knows solutions exist, evaluating the category | Category positioning + differentiators | Feature depth, comparison tables, social proof by authority |
| **Product-Aware** | Knows this product, comparing against alternatives | Specific advantages + switching logic | vs. pages, migration support, switcher testimonials |
| **Most-Aware** | Ready to buy, needs final push | Risk reversal + friction removal | Pricing, trial signup, demo booking |

### Category-Unaware (Extended)

The visitor doesn't know the problem category exists. They experience symptoms but have no mental framework for the solution — they cannot search for solutions because they don't know what to search for.

**Common in:** Novel tooling categories, emerging software categories, products creating new markets.

**Opening strategy:** Lead with a *symptom headline* — describe the pain the visitor already feels, then name the category as the solution frame. Example: Instead of "The leading observability platform" → "Your production incidents take 4x longer to resolve than they should."

**Framework:** Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) replaces awareness levels. The site's task is to connect the visitor's job to a solution category, then to the product. Category creation frameworks (Play Bigger's "Category Design") also apply.

**Page structure implications:** Longer educational content above the fold. "How it works" does double duty as category education. Blog and content marketing become the primary acquisition channel.

## Dual-Persona Routing Principle

B2B websites frequently serve two distinct persona types who evaluate using different cognitive frameworks. The site must detect the visitor's evaluation mode and route accordingly — not force both through the same funnel.

**Pattern:** Identify the two dominant personas, map their distinct evaluation criteria, and provide explicit routing (navigation segmentation, role-selector patterns, or content that addresses both in sequence).

**Domain manifestations:**
- b2b-saas: Technical evaluator vs. business buyer (enterprise evaluation committee)
- b2b-services: Decision-maker evaluating expertise vs. procurement evaluating credentials
- b2b-industrial: Engineer evaluating specs vs. procurement evaluating compliance/pricing

## Conversion Triggers

| Trigger | Mechanism | Where it appears |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Social proof specificity | Named companies + quantified results > "thousands of customers" | Trust bar, testimonials, case studies |
| Risk reversal | Free trial, no credit card, money-back guarantee | Hero CTA micro-copy, pricing page |
| Time-to-value visibility | "Setup in 5 minutes" / "See results in first week" | How It Works, hero subhead |
| Peer validation | "Teams your size use this" / industry-specific proof | Social proof sections, enterprise page |
| Urgency (when genuine) | Limited pilot slots, price lock, compliance deadline | Mid-page CTA, pricing page |

## "Coming from X" — Cross-Domain Warnings

When a B2B project team has experience primarily in other site types, these transfer errors are common:

| Coming from... | Watch for... |
|----------------|--------------|
| **E-commerce** | Over-optimizing for immediate conversion. B2B sales cycles are longer; the site must nurture, not just convert. Don't apply e-commerce urgency tactics (countdown timers, "only 3 left") to B2B — it destroys credibility. |
| **Portfolio** | Under-investing in social proof. Portfolio sites rely on the work speaking for itself; B2B requires explicit trust signals (logos, metrics, testimonials). Don't assume "our product screenshots will sell it." |
| **Media / Content** | Prioritizing content volume over conversion paths. Media sites optimize for pageviews and engagement; B2B sites must route visitors toward a conversion action. Every content section needs a CTA escape hatch. |
| **Local Business** | Assuming a single decision-maker. Local business sites target one person making a quick decision; B2B involves committees, evaluation cycles, and stakeholder-specific content needs. |
| **Consumer App** | Oversimplifying the message. Consumer apps sell aspiration with minimal copy; B2B requires depth, specificity, and multi-stakeholder messaging. "Find your calm" works for Headspace; B2B needs "Reduce invoice processing time by 62%." |
