# B2B: Discovery

Domain knowledge shared across all B2B site types.
Part of: references/domains/b2b/

---

## B2B Messaging Hierarchy

The sequence that answers the user's questions in order of importance.

| Layer | Question | Validation Question | Where to Address |
|-------|----------|---------------------|------------------|
| **1. Clarity** | "What is it?" | *Can they identify the category in 5s?* | **Hero H1**: Plain language (e.g., "Invoice Automation Software"). |
| **2. Relevance** | "Is this for me?" | *Do they see their role/pain?* | **Subhead/Nav**: Industry or role-based terms. |
| **3. Value** | "What do I get?" | *Is the outcome desirable enough?* | **Body Sections**: Focus on outcomes (time saved, revenue gained). |
| **4. Differentiation** | "Why you over X?" | *Is the delta distinct and provable?* | **Comparison Block**: State advantages explicitly. |
| **5. Action** | "What now?" | *Is the next step obvious?* | **CTA**: Clear, low-friction next step. |

## Rules
- **Clarity beats cleverness**: "We leverage synergies" → "We save you 10 hours/week".
- **Value Prop Dominance**: The Value Prop must be the most visually dominant element on the page.
- **Write copy BEFORE design**: The messaging dictates the architecture, not the other way around.

## B2B Discovery Guidance

### Key Assumptions to Form
- **Sales motion**: Self-serve / consultative / hybrid?
- **Buyer vs user**: Same person or different? (affects navigation strategy)
- **Buying committee**: How many stakeholders? (affects content depth)
- **Competitive landscape**: Established category or novel?
- **Primary channel**: Organic / paid / outbound / partner?

---

## Unique Constraints
- **Security pages**: Required for enterprise buyers (SOC2, GDPR, ISO)
- **Pricing transparency**: Expected in self-serve; optional in enterprise/consultative

## Regulated Industry Guidance

Fintech, healthcare, and legaltech do not require separate archetypes — they add a compliance layer on top of existing archetypes:

| Concern | What changes | Implementation |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Compliance disclosures | Footer expands with required legal language, cookie consent more prominent, terms linked from pricing/signup | Compliance footer component variant |
| Trust signals | Security certifications (SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI DSS) move from About page to hero-adjacent | Elevate trust bar with cert badges |
| Secondary nav for legal | Regulatory content (privacy policy, DPA, compliance docs) needs its own nav section | Utility nav cluster or footer mega-section |
| Gated features | Some product screenshots or pricing may be restricted pre-NDA | "Request Access" pattern variant on pricing page |

## Specialization-as-Positioning Principle

B2B businesses that position around a narrow specialization convert at higher rates than generalists. The website must communicate specialization clearly within the first 5 seconds — hero headline, navigation labels, and proof points should all reinforce the niche.

**Framework:** Category → Specialization → Qualifiers (the "Waterfall of Differentiation"). Each level narrows the competitive set. A website that fails to establish this hierarchy forces visitors to parse vague claims.

**Discovery questions to assess specialization:**
- How narrow is the business's focus? (horizontal platform vs. vertical specialist)
- Does the business serve a specific industry, role, or problem?
- Can the hero headline pass the "5-second specificity test" — would a visitor know exactly what the business does and for whom?

**Domain manifestations:**
- b2b-saas: Vertical SaaS vs. horizontal platform positioning
- b2b-services: Deep niche expertise vs. full-service agency
- b2b-industrial: Specialized manufacturer vs. general-purpose supplier
