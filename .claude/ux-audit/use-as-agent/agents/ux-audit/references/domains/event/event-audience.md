# Event: Audience Psychology

Domain knowledge for event / product launch sites.
Part of: references/domains/event/

---

## Urgency-Modulated Decision Arc

Event visitors operate on an urgency-modulated decision arc:

| Stage | Visitor State | Primary Need | Conversion Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Intrigued** | "What is this event about?" | Core value prop in 5 seconds: what, who, when, where, why attend | Clear event summary above fold |
| **Evaluating** | "Is this worth my time/money?" | Speakers, agenda, pricing | Speaker quality + relevant content |
| **Justifying** | "Can I justify this to myself/my boss?" | ROI argument, past testimonials | Specific session outcomes, peer proof |
| **Urgency-triggered** | "I need to act now or lose out" | Countdown, early-bird deadline, limited seats | Scarcity + deadline mechanics |
| **Registering** | "Make this quick" | Short, frictionless registration form | Name + email + ticket type (minimal) |

### Time/Cost Justification
The primary barrier is NOT "Is this event good?" but "Is this worth my time?" (free events) or "Is this worth the money?" (paid events). Events compete against the visitor's calendar, not against competitor events.

### The 78/76 Rule
78% of attendees prioritize learning opportunities; 76% value networking. Implication: the agenda and speaker lineup are the two highest-impact sections on any event page.

### Urgency Mechanics
Countdown timers with genuine deadlines drive up to 332% conversion lifts. But fake countdowns that reset destroy trust permanently. Urgency must be real: early-bird pricing, limited seats, registration close date.

## Conversion Triggers

| Trigger | Mechanism | Where it appears |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Countdown timer | Real deadline creates action pressure | Hero, closing CTA |
| Speaker credibility | Quality of speakers = quality of event | Speaker section |
| Early-bird pricing | Price advantage for early commitment | Ticket tier section |
| Social proof of attendance | "1,247 people registered" | Hero, trust section |
| Specific value promises | "Connect with 50+ VPs of Product" not "great networking" | Value prop section |
| Past event proof | Photos, recordings, testimonials from previous edition | Social proof section |

## "Coming from X" — Cross-Domain Warnings

| Coming from... | Watch for... |
|----------------|--------------|
| **B2B SaaS** | Building a multi-page feature tour instead of a single-purpose conversion page. Events need one page, one goal. Don't create "How It Works," "Platform," or "Resources" sections — they dilute the registration CTA. |
| **E-commerce** | Applying product-catalog thinking to sessions/workshops. An agenda is not a product grid — editorial hierarchy matters. Keynotes are not equal to breakout sessions. |
| **Portfolio** | Over-designing at the expense of information density. Event pages need to communicate what/who/when/where/how-much quickly. Visual artistry should support, not replace, information clarity. |
| **Media / Content** | Prioritizing content depth over conversion. A blog post about the event topic is less valuable than a strong speaker lineup and clear pricing. Content supports marketing, not the event page itself. |
| **Nonprofit** | Applying empathy-first storytelling to a time-bound event. Conferences don't need a "problem section" — attendees already know why they want to attend. Lead with speakers and agenda, not mission statements. |
