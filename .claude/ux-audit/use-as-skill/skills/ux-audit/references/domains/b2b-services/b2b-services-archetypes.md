# B2B Services: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for B2B professional services sites.
Part of: references/domains/b2b-services/

---

Archetypes define what a services site optimizes for at the architectural level. Unlike product companies, service firms sell intangible outcomes delivered by people — the archetype determines which trust mechanism leads.

Most successful firms blend archetypes. The critical decision is which leads (homepage, above-the-fold) vs. which supports (deeper in site).

---

## Credibility-Led (Expertise-First)
**Optimizes for:** Intellectual authority and trust as the primary conversion mechanism. The firm's reputation, credentials, and published thinking do the selling.
**Best fit:** Management consultancies, law firms, accounting firms, advisory practices — firms where the buyer is C-suite or senior leadership competing on reputation and intellectual capital. Firms where 73% of buyers arrive via referral and need validation, not discovery.
**Sacrifices:** Visual impact; portfolio showcase; conversion speed. Clean typographic design projects "quiet confidence" but may feel austere to visually-oriented buyers.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage hero focuses on outcomes and strategic positioning, not service descriptions
- Navigation: Insights/Thinking prominent; massive content section serves as primary investment
- Key pages: Insights hub (articles, reports, original research), Team with deep credential bios, Case Studies framed as "Client Stories" emphasizing partnership narrative
- Social proof: Named client logos, media appearances, publications, speaking engagements, professional accreditations
- Design: Clean, typographic, minimalist — projects quiet authority

---

## Showcase-Led (Work-First)
**Optimizes for:** Portfolio quality as the primary proof mechanism. The work speaks for itself — visitors evaluate capability through output, not claims.
**Best fit:** Creative agencies, architecture studios, design firms, branding agencies — firms where output is visual or experiential, and prospects need to see quality of work before engaging. The website itself functions as a portfolio piece.
**Sacrifices:** Thought leadership depth; content marketing as acquisition channel. Emphasis on visuals can underserve procurement evaluators who need credentials and process documentation.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage built around project showcases with high-impact visuals
- Navigation: Work/Projects first; Services secondary
- Key pages: Immersive long-form case studies (visual narratives, not metric-driven), Awards/Recognition section, Filtered project gallery (by industry, service type, outcome)
- Social proof: Award badges from recognized industry programs, "As seen in" media logos, third-party review platform ratings (Clutch, Awwwards)
- Design: Cutting-edge with animations and custom interactions — the site IS the portfolio

---

## Methodology-Led (Process-First)
**Optimizes for:** Proprietary process as the differentiator. Reduces perceived risk by making the intangible tangible — visitors can see exactly what happens after signing.
**Best fit:** Implementation consulting, operational consulting, training firms, firms differentiating through repeatable named processes. Firms where the buyer's primary anxiety is "what will I actually get?"
**Sacrifices:** Flexibility perception — a rigid process may deter clients with non-standard needs. May undersell the people behind the process.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage hero introduces proprietary methodology or framework by name
- Navigation: Process/Methodology prominent; structured engagement model visible
- Key pages: Visual process diagrams with phases (Audit → Strategy → Implementation → Review), Service pages detail deliverables and process stages rather than just capabilities, Engagement Model page showing team makeup per phase
- Social proof: Methodology-specific results ("Clients using our [Framework] see 3x improvement in...")
- Design: Structured, diagrammatic — process visualizations as primary content type
- Pricing may be "productized" into clear tiers tied to methodology stages

---

## Relationship-Led (People-First)
**Optimizes for:** Team chemistry and cultural fit as the primary selection criterion. The individual consultant or team IS the product — hiring the firm means hiring these specific people.
**Best fit:** Executive coaching, boutique advisory practices, recruitment consultancies, smaller firms where individual consultant relationships drive retention and referrals. Firms where cultural fit matters as much as capability.
**Sacrifices:** Scalability signals — emphasizing personal relationships can make the firm seem small or dependent on specific individuals. May deter enterprise procurement teams who need institutional credibility.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage features founder story and team prominently above the fold
- Navigation: Team/People as primary entry point; About elevated
- Key pages: Extensive "Meet the Team" with deep profiles (publications, speaking, personal details), Founder story page, Testimonials emphasizing collaboration quality ("Working with [name] felt like...")
- Social proof: Relationship-focused testimonials quoting quality of collaboration, not just outcomes
- Design: Warm, approachable, personality-driven over corporate authority

---

## Archetype Selection

When choosing an archetype for a services firm:

1. **What does the buyer evaluate first?** Credentials → Credibility-Led. Output quality → Showcase-Led. Process clarity → Methodology-Led. People/chemistry → Relationship-Led.
2. **How do clients typically find the firm?** Referral-heavy (73% of B2B execs) → Credibility-Led or Relationship-Led (site validates warm impression). Cold/search-heavy → Showcase-Led or Methodology-Led (site must create trust from zero).
3. **What is the buyer's primary anxiety?** "Are they experts?" → Credibility-Led. "Can they deliver quality?" → Showcase-Led. "What will I actually get?" → Methodology-Led. "Will we work well together?" → Relationship-Led.

**Default combinations:**
- Management consultancy / law firm / advisory: Credibility-Led
- Creative agency / design studio / architecture: Showcase-Led
- Implementation / process consulting / training: Methodology-Led
- Executive coaching / boutique advisory / cultural fit-driven: Relationship-Led
