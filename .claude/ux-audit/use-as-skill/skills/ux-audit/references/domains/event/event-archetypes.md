# Event: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for event / product launch sites.
Part of: references/domains/event/

---

Archetypes define what a site optimizes for at the architectural level — how pages connect, what narrative the site tells, and which user journey is prioritized.

**These are SITE-LEVEL archetypes** (what the overall site structure optimizes for). They are distinct from PAGE-LEVEL layout patterns in `patterns.md`, which define the internal structure of individual pages.

**Key distinction from B2B:** Events are time-bound (creating natural urgency), social proof is speaker/attendee-based (not customer logos), and CTA friction varies dramatically (free RSVP vs. $2,000 conference ticket). B2B SaaS feature-benefit-demo flow doesn't work for events.

---

## Urgency-Driven (Time-Bound)
**Optimizes for:** Registration/signup rate within a shrinking time window.
**Best fit:** Conferences, workshops, webinars, product launches, crowdfunding campaigns, waitlist pages, seasonal sales events, time-limited offers — any page with a deadline.
**Sacrifices:** Evergreen content, SEO depth, ongoing engagement. This is a single-purpose, time-limited page that becomes irrelevant after the date passes.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with event name + date/time + location + countdown timer + "Register Now" CTA — all above fold
- Navigation: Single page or minimal. One page, one goal. Brands that stripped extra buttons and kept a single CTA saw up to 3.7× increase in signups
- Key pages: Landing page (often single-page), Speakers/Lineup, Agenda/Schedule, Tickets/Pricing, FAQ, Venue/Travel
- Social proof: Speaker headshots + credentials, past attendee testimonials, attendee/company logos, "X people registered" counter, media partner logos

---

## Community-Event (Recurring Series)
**Optimizes for:** Recurring attendance and community building — the event series becomes a habit.
**Best fit:** Monthly meetups, recurring webinar series, annual conferences with year-round community, cohort-based workshops, hackathons.
**Sacrifices:** Single-event urgency. The site needs to balance current event promotion with series identity and past event proof.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with next event details + "Join the Community" CTA alongside event registration
- Navigation: Next Event, Past Events, Community, About, Register
- Key pages: Current event page, Past events archive (with recordings/recaps), Community page, Speaker archive
- Social proof: Total attendee count across events, returning attendee rate, community member count, testimonials from multiple events

---

## Launch-Page (Product/Campaign)
**Optimizes for:** Waitlist signups, pre-orders, or launch-day conversion — a time-limited product page rather than an event page.
**Best fit:** Product launches, Kickstarter/Indiegogo campaigns, app launches, "coming soon" pages, limited-edition drops.
**Sacrifices:** Ongoing content, detailed product information. The page creates anticipation, not education.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with product teaser + countdown to launch + email signup for notification
- Navigation: Often none (single-page) or minimal: Features, Pricing, FAQ
- Key pages: Landing page (single-page), FAQ, Pre-order/Waitlist
- Social proof: Waitlist count ("12,000 people waiting"), early access testimonials, press coverage, investor logos

---

## Hybrid (Conference + Year-Round)
**Optimizes for:** Event registration AND year-round engagement — the conference is the anchor, but the brand persists between events.
**Best fit:** Major industry conferences (Web Summit, SaaStr), professional association events, multi-day conferences with year-round content programs.
**Sacrifices:** Single-page simplicity. The site is a full content platform with an event attached.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage pivots between "event mode" (6 weeks before event) and "content mode" (rest of year)
- Navigation: Event (date, speakers, tickets), Content (blog, podcast, videos), Community, About
- Key pages: Event hub, Speaker directory, Agenda, Tickets, Blog/Content, Community, About
- Social proof: Past event stats, content library, speaker alumni, media partnerships

---

## Archetype Selection

When choosing an archetype:

1. **Frequency** — One-time or recurring?
2. **Time horizon** — Is the deadline weeks away or months?
3. **Year-round presence** — Does the brand exist between events?

**Default combinations:**
- One-time conference or workshop: Urgency-Driven
- Monthly meetup or webinar series: Community-Event
- Product launch or crowdfunding: Launch-Page
- Major annual conference with year-round content: Hybrid
