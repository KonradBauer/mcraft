# Community: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for community and membership sites.
Part of: references/domains/community/

---

Archetypes define what a community site optimizes for at the architectural level — how pages connect, what narrative the site tells, and which member journey is prioritized.

**These are SITE-LEVEL archetypes** (what the overall site structure optimizes for). They are distinct from PAGE-LEVEL layout patterns in `patterns.md`, which define the internal structure of individual pages.

---

## Creator-Led Community
**Optimizes for:** Member signups (free or paid); core metric is member activation rate (first meaningful engagement, not just signup).
**Best fit:** Creator communities (Patreon, Circle, Mighty Networks), professional networks (specialized Slack/Discord groups), membership programs (premium newsletters with community access), fan communities.
**Sacrifices:** Content preview depth — must protect walled-garden value while showing enough to justify joining. Core product (member interactions) is inherently gated.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with belonging statement: "Join 5,000+ [identity group] who [shared activity]"
- Navigation is thin pre-join: What's Inside, Membership Tiers, About, FAQ
- Post-join navigation transforms to in-community experience (forums, events, content)
- Social proof: member count, active metrics ("500+ posts this week"), member testimonials about belonging, creator credentials

---

## Professional Network
**Optimizes for:** Professional connection value and knowledge exchange.
**Best fit:** Industry-specific communities, mastermind groups, alumni networks.
**Sacrifices:** Casual accessibility — requires professional identity verification or qualification criteria.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage leads with network value proposition: the network IS the product (not content, not the creator)
- Navigation: Member directory, events, knowledge base, application/join
- Key pages: Member benefits, directory preview, upcoming events, application form
- Social proof: member titles and companies, industry representation, outcome stories ("Found my co-founder here")

---

## Interest-Based Community
**Optimizes for:** Engagement volume and content generation by members.
**Best fit:** Hobby communities, fan groups, local communities.
**Sacrifices:** Monetization clarity — value is in participation, not a clear product.

**Site structure signal:**
- Open or low-barrier membership (no application, minimal friction)
- Homepage showcases recent activity and trending discussions
- Navigation: Forums/topics, events, member projects, join
- Social proof: activity volume, member-created content highlights, community milestones
