# Community: Audience Psychology

Domain knowledge for community and membership sites.
Part of: references/domains/community/

---

## Belonging Readiness Model

The standard B2B awareness model does not map cleanly to community sites. Community audiences are better modeled by **belonging readiness** — a progression from curiosity to active participation.

| Level | Visitor state | Opening strategy | Primary content |
|-------|---------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Curious** | Heard about the community, browsing casually | Identity recognition — "This is for people like me" | Belonging hero, community description, sample content preview |
| **Evaluating** | Interested but needs proof it's worth joining | Activity proof + exclusivity framing | Active metrics, member testimonials, "what members get" breakdown |
| **Convinced** | Ready to join, needs clear path | Friction removal + tier clarity | Signup flow, membership tiers, FAQ, "cancel anytime" reassurance |
| **Active Member** | Already inside, deepening engagement | Engagement hooks + progression | In-community navigation, notifications, member milestones |

## Audience Segments

### Belonging-Seeker
**Motivation:** Identity-driven. "I want to connect with people like me."
**Evaluates:** Community culture, member quality, shared values, tone of discussions.
**Key question:** "Will I feel like I belong here?"
**Content needs:** Member stories, community values, culture signals, identity-specific language.

### Value-Seeker
**Motivation:** Content/expertise-driven. "I want access to exclusive content/expertise."
**Evaluates:** Content quality, exclusivity, practical takeaways, expert access.
**Key question:** "Is there enough valuable content to justify the cost?"
**Content needs:** Content previews, expert credentials, content library overview, sample resources.

### Creator Fan
**Motivation:** Follows a specific creator. Will join wherever the creator goes.
**Evaluates:** Creator accessibility within the community — can they interact directly?
**Key question:** "Will I actually get closer to [Creator]?"
**Content needs:** Creator presence signals, AMA schedules, direct interaction opportunities, creator-exclusive content.

## Primary Barrier

"Is this community actually active, or will I pay and find a ghost town?"

Activity proof is the single most important trust signal for community sites. Unlike product sites where the product speaks for itself, a community's value is invisible until you're inside. Every landing page must answer: "This community is alive, right now."

**Browsing → Acting trigger:** Evidence of recent, genuine member activity + clear articulation of "what members get that non-members don't."

## Cross-Domain Warnings (D13)

### Coming from B2B SaaS
Community conversion is not sales funnel optimization. Over-optimizing for signup without activation creates ghost towns. The metric that matters is engagement, not MRR. A community with 200 active members outperforms one with 2,000 inactive signups.

### Coming from Media
Community content is member-generated, not editorially controlled. Quality variance is a feature (authenticity), not a bug. Attempting editorial control kills the organic engagement that makes communities valuable.

### Coming from E-commerce
There's no "product page" — the product is invisible until you're inside. Trust must be built with activity proof and testimonials, not product images. The closest equivalent to a product demo is a sample content preview with engagement metrics visible.
