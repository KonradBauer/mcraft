# Media: Audience Psychology

Domain knowledge for media / content publication sites.
Part of: references/domains/media/

---

## Consumption-Habit Loop

Media visitors operate on a consumption-habit loop, not an awareness funnel:

| Stage | Visitor State | Primary Need | Engagement Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Scan** | "What's new? What's interesting?" | Headlines, topic labels, freshness signals | Relevant category, compelling headline |
| **Select** | "This headline interests me" | Headline + image + topic + reading time estimate | Trust in author + interest match |
| **Consume** | "I'm reading this article" | Clean reading experience, no disruptive ads | Content quality + readability |
| **Continue** | "What else is worth reading?" | Related articles, trending lists, "next" prompts | Content recommendations |
| **Return** | "I should come back" | Newsletter, bookmarking, habit cues | Subscription + consistent quality |

### F-Pattern Scanning
Homepage visitors scan in an F-pattern: horizontal across the top (featured article), horizontal across the next row (secondary stories), then vertical down the left side (headlines only). Implication: the most important content must be top-left, and subsequent content must lead with the headline.

### Article-First Arrival
Most visitors arrive at an article page from search or social — NOT the homepage. The article page IS the first impression. Homepage design matters less than article page design for most media sites.

### Primary Barrier
Information overload and attention competition. Every article competes with every other article on the internet. Content quality and presentation clarity are existential.

## Conversion Triggers

| Trigger | Mechanism | Where it appears |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Headline quality | Specific value promise, not clickbait | Article cards, social shares |
| Freshness | Recent publication date, "Latest" label | Article cards, homepage |
| Author trust | Named author with credentials and track record | Author bio, byline |
| Reading time | "8 min read" — sets commitment expectation | Article cards, article header |
| Social proof of popularity | "Trending," view counts, "Most Read" | Sidebar, homepage sections |
| Newsletter value prop | "Get the best of [Publication] weekly" | Inline subscribe, footer |

## "Coming from X" — Cross-Domain Warnings

| Coming from... | Watch for... |
|----------------|--------------|
| **B2B SaaS** | Treating content as a lead gen tool instead of the product itself. On a media site, the article IS the value — don't interrupt it with CTAs, gated content, or "Book a Demo" buttons. Respect the reading experience. |
| **E-commerce** | Applying product-catalog thinking. Articles aren't products in a grid. Editorial hierarchy matters — not every article is equal. Feature the best content, don't list everything equally. |
| **Portfolio** | Over-curating. Media sites need volume and freshness, not a curated selection of 5 pieces. Daily or weekly publishing rhythm matters more than perfection. |
| **Nonprofit** | Adding emotional CTAs to every article. Readers come for information, not to be guilted into action. Save the donation ask for the right moment. |
| **Local Business** | Applying phone-first, location-prominent patterns. Media sites are consumed globally. Geographic signals are irrelevant unless it's a hyperlocal publication. |
