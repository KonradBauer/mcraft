# Media: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for media / content publication sites.
Part of: references/domains/media/

---

Archetypes define what a site optimizes for at the architectural level — how pages connect, what narrative the site tells, and which user journey is prioritized.

**These are SITE-LEVEL archetypes** (what the overall site structure optimizes for). They are distinct from PAGE-LEVEL layout patterns in `patterns.md`, which define the internal structure of individual pages.

**Key distinction from B2B:** On a media site, content IS the product. On B2B, content is the vehicle to the product. Media monetizes attention directly (ads, subscriptions); B2B monetizes the trust content builds. This changes every design decision.

---

## Content-First (Publication)
**Optimizes for:** Pages per session × return visit rate (engagement depth × habit formation).
**Best fit:** Online magazines, news publications, blogs with ad/subscription revenue, newsletter-as-product businesses, niche media brands — any site where content is the value.
**Sacrifices:** Lead generation, product conversion, sales funnel optimization. The site keeps visitors reading and returning, not moving toward a purchase.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with editorial layout — featured article with large image + headline, grid of recent articles, topic navigation prominent
- Navigation: Category-driven ("Technology," "Culture," "Business," "Opinion"), search prominent, no "Products" or "Pricing"
- Key pages: Homepage (editorial feed), Category pages, Article pages, Author pages, About/Masthead, Newsletter, Archive/Search
- Social proof: Article view counts, author credentials, "Trending" labels, media awards, subscriber count

---

## Newsletter-First (Creator Publication)
**Optimizes for:** Email subscriber conversion and retention — the newsletter IS the product, the website is the archive and conversion engine.
**Best fit:** Substack-model publications, independent journalists, single-author newsletters with paid tiers, industry-specific digests (Morning Brew, The Hustle model).
**Sacrifices:** Website engagement depth. Pages per session matters less because primary consumption happens in email, not on-site.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with newsletter value prop + email subscribe form above the fold — the site is a landing page for the newsletter
- Navigation: Minimal — Archive, About, Subscribe. Often single-page
- Key pages: Homepage/subscribe page, Archive (past issues), About the author, Paid tier info
- Social proof: Subscriber count, testimonials from readers, "As read by teams at [companies]"

---

## Community-Editorial (Forum Hybrid)
**Optimizes for:** User participation and community-generated content alongside editorial content.
**Best fit:** Niche media with active reader communities, industry publications with user contributions, platforms combining editorial and UGC (Product Hunt, Hacker News model).
**Sacrifices:** Editorial control. User content may vary in quality; moderation is a cost.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage mixes editorial and community content — "Editor's Picks" alongside "Trending in Community"
- Navigation: Editorial sections + Community/Forum/Discussions
- Key pages: Homepage, Article pages, Discussion threads, User profiles, Submit/Contribute
- Social proof: User participation metrics, contributor count, upvote/karma systems

---

## Multimedia / Video-First
**Optimizes for:** Watch time and video engagement — video content is primary, text supports it.
**Best fit:** Video-native publications, YouTube channels with companion websites, podcast networks with video, documentary series, educational video platforms.
**Sacrifices:** Text-first reading experience. The site is a video player with context, not a reading platform.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with featured video + video grid, autoplay (muted) hero
- Navigation: Shows/Series, Topics, Live, About
- Key pages: Homepage (video feed), Show/Series pages, Individual video pages, Live events, About
- Social proof: View counts, subscriber count, episode count

---

## Archetype Selection

When choosing an archetype:

1. **Revenue model** — Ad-supported? Subscription? Newsletter with paid tier? Sponsorship?
2. **Content format** — Written articles? Newsletter emails? Video? Community-generated?
3. **Publishing frequency** — Daily (freshness-focused)? Weekly/monthly (evergreen-focused)?

**Default combinations:**
- Online magazine / news site: Content-First
- Independent journalist / creator: Newsletter-First
- Industry community with editorial: Community-Editorial
- Video-native publication: Multimedia / Video-First
