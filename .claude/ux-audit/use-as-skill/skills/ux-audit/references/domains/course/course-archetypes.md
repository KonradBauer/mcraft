# Course: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for online course and education product sites.
Part of: references/domains/course/

---

Archetypes define what a site optimizes for at the architectural level — how pages connect, what narrative the site tells, and which user journey is prioritized.

**These are SITE-LEVEL archetypes** (what the overall site structure optimizes for). They are distinct from PAGE-LEVEL layout patterns in `patterns.md`, which define the internal structure of individual pages.

---

## Solo Creator Course
**Optimizes for:** Enrollment (purchase or signup). Core metric: landing-page-to-enrollment conversion rate.
**Best fit:** Individual creator courses (Teachable, Podia), skill-specific workshops.
**Sacrifices:** Ongoing engagement signaling — no "see it in action" equivalent. The product is consumed after purchase, so all trust must be built pre-purchase.

**Key principle:** Must sell a transformation, not a list of modules.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with transformation headline — "Become [outcome]" not "Learn [topic]"
- Navigation minimal: Curriculum, Instructor, Testimonials, Pricing, FAQ
- Social proof: student outcome metrics, enrollment count, instructor credentials, before-after stories
- Key pages: Landing page (often the only page), Curriculum detail, Instructor bio, Checkout

---

## Platform Course
**Optimizes for:** Course discovery and enrollment across multiple instructors.
**Best fit:** Education platforms (MasterClass, Domestika, Udemy, Coursera).
**Sacrifices:** Single-instructor relationship — the platform brand competes with instructor identity.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage is a catalog with category browse and search
- Navigation: Categories, Trending, My Learning, Search
- Social proof: platform-level metrics (total students, course count), per-course ratings and enrollment counts
- Key pages: Category browse, Course detail, Instructor profile, Learning dashboard

---

## Cohort-Based Program
**Optimizes for:** Cohort enrollment within a specific time window.
**Best fit:** Cohort-based courses (Maven, Reforge), bootcamps, live workshops.
**Sacrifices:** Evergreen availability — urgency is built into the model.

**Key differentiator:** Enrollment deadline creates natural urgency. Unlike evergreen courses, the time constraint is real and legitimate.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage leads with next cohort date and enrollment deadline
- Navigation: Program, Instructor, Alumni Outcomes, Apply
- Social proof emphasizes alumni outcomes and cohort community
- Key pages: Program landing, Application form, Alumni showcase, Cohort calendar
