# Course: Audience Psychology

Domain knowledge for online course and education product sites.
Part of: references/domains/course/

---

## Visitor Personas

### Aspiration-Driven Learner
Wants transformation, willing to invest. Evaluates outcomes over content volume. Core question: "Will this actually change my career?" Responds to: specific outcome metrics, before-after student stories, curriculum that maps to a clear skill progression. Will pay premium if transformation is convincingly demonstrated.

### Comparison Shopper
Evaluating this course vs alternatives — including free content. Needs justification for premium pricing. Core question: "Why pay when YouTube exists?" Responds to: structured learning path vs scattered free content, instructor access, community, certificate, accountability. Must see what premium adds beyond raw information.

### Credential Seeker
Wants certificate, portfolio piece, or career credential. Evaluates recognition value. Less about learning, more about proof. Core question: "Will employers/clients recognize this?" Responds to: certificate details, employer recognition, portfolio project quality, alumni career outcomes. May skim curriculum but closely examines credential section.

### Returning Learner
Took previous course from this creator/platform. Already trusts. Core question: "Is this different enough from what I already know?" Responds to: clear differentiation from previous courses, advanced curriculum preview, loyalty pricing. Lower barrier to conversion but higher content expectations.

---

## Arrival Mental State

Aspiration mixed with skepticism. The visitor wants the transformation but has been burned by or is aware of low-quality courses. Primary barrier: **value uncertainty** — "Is this worth the price when free content exists?"

---

## Browsing → Acting Trigger

The shift from browsing to enrollment happens when three signals converge:
1. **Specific, quantified outcomes** from past students (not vague praise)
2. **Clear curriculum** showing a structured learning path (not just topic list)
3. **Instructor credibility** justifying premium over free alternatives

All three are necessary. Missing any one creates enough doubt to delay purchase.

---

## Awareness Model

Most visitors arrive **solution-aware** (they know courses exist as a solution) or **product-aware** (they know this specific course). The jump from solution-aware to purchase is longer than in other domains because courses compete directly with free alternatives (YouTube, blog posts, documentation).

| Level | Visitor knows... | Opening strategy |
|-------|------------------|------------------|
| **Problem-Aware** | Has a skill gap but hasn't considered a course | Lead with the outcome/transformation, then introduce course as the vehicle |
| **Solution-Aware** | Knows courses are an option, comparing formats | Differentiate: structured path vs scattered free content, accountability, community |
| **Product-Aware** | Knows this specific course, evaluating | Proof: outcomes, curriculum depth, instructor credentials, guarantee |
| **Most-Aware** | Ready to buy, needs final push | Risk reversal: money-back guarantee, payment plans, enrollment urgency |

## Cross-Domain Warnings (D13)

### Coming from B2B SaaS
Course conversion is one-time, not subscription. There is no "free trial" equivalent — the product is consumed after purchase. Trust must be built entirely pre-purchase through previews, curriculum transparency, and guarantees. Do not design for ongoing engagement funnels.

### Coming from E-Commerce
The "product" is intangible. There is no unboxing, no physical proof of quality. Transformation testimonials replace product reviews. Social proof must demonstrate outcome, not satisfaction. "Great course!" is worthless — "I landed a new role 3 months after completing this" is everything.

### Coming from Media
Course content is NOT free-to-sample like articles. The paywall is before consumption, not after engagement. Preview strategy is completely different — you cannot hook with content and then gate. Instead, you must sell the promise of content with curriculum previews, sample lessons, and outcome proof.
