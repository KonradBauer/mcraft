# Local Business: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for local business sites.
Part of: references/domains/local-business/

---

Archetypes define what a site optimizes for at the architectural level — how pages connect, what narrative the site tells, and which user journey is prioritized.

**These are SITE-LEVEL archetypes** (what the overall site structure optimizes for). They are distinct from PAGE-LEVEL layout patterns in `patterns.md`, which define the internal structure of individual pages.

---

## Proximity-Action (Local Service)
**Optimizes for:** Contact actions per visit (phone calls, appointment bookings, direction requests, online orders).
**Best fit:** Restaurants, dental/medical clinics, gyms, law firms, plumbers, electricians, salons, auto repair shops — any business serving a geographic area with in-person delivery.
**Sacrifices:** Content depth, brand storytelling, educational content. The site assumes the visitor already knows they need the service — they're choosing which provider, not whether to buy.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with "[Service] in [Location]" headline + click-to-call button visible above fold on mobile + hours/address visible without scrolling
- Navigation: 5-7 items max — Home, Services, About, Reviews, Contact. CTA button ("Call Now" / "Book Appointment") in top-right of header on every page
- Key pages: Homepage, individual Service pages, About, Testimonials/Reviews, Contact, Service Areas
- Social proof: Google review widget (star rating + review count + quotes), before/after photos, credentials/licenses, "Years in business"

---

## Booking-First (Appointment Business)
**Optimizes for:** Online booking completion rate — reducing phone call dependency.
**Best fit:** Salons, spas, dental offices, medical clinics, fitness studios, tutoring services — businesses where appointments are the primary revenue model.
**Sacrifices:** Browsing experience. The site channels every visitor toward the booking system as fast as possible.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with "Book Now" as the dominant CTA, often with embedded booking widget above the fold
- Navigation: "Book Online" appears in header as primary CTA on every page
- Key pages: Homepage, Services + Pricing, Team/Providers, Book Online, Reviews, Contact
- Social proof: Google reviews, provider-specific ratings, "X appointments booked this month"

---

## Menu-First (Food & Hospitality)
**Optimizes for:** Menu access speed and order/reservation conversion.
**Best fit:** Restaurants, cafés, bars, bakeries, catering companies — any food service business where the menu IS the primary content.
**Sacrifices:** Everything that's not menu, hours, location, and ordering. The site is a menu with a reservation button.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with food imagery hero + "Order Online" / "Make a Reservation" dual CTA
- Navigation: Menu, Order Online, Reservations, Location & Hours, Catering (optional)
- Key pages: Menu page (THE most visited page), Order Online, Reservations, Location/Hours, About/Story (optional)
- Social proof: Yelp/Google ratings, food photography, press mentions ("Best Pizza in Chicago — Chicago Tribune")

---

## Multi-Location (Chain/Franchise)
**Optimizes for:** Location findability — routing visitors to their nearest location's page.
**Best fit:** Chains with 3+ locations, franchises, multi-office professional practices, bank branches, retail chains.
**Sacrifices:** Single-location personality. The site becomes a directory with brand consistency, not a personal local presence.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with location finder (zip code input or "Find Near Me" using geolocation)
- Navigation: Locations, Services, About, Careers, Contact
- Key pages: Location finder, individual Location pages (each with hours, address, phone, reviews, team), Services, About
- Social proof: Total location count, aggregate reviews, brand recognition

---

## Archetype Selection

When choosing an archetype:

1. **Primary conversion action** — Phone call? Online booking? Dine-in reservation? Online order?
2. **Location count** — Single location or multiple?
3. **Service type** — Appointment-based? Walk-in? Delivery/takeout?

**Default combinations:**
- Plumber, electrician, contractor: Proximity-Action
- Salon, dentist, fitness studio: Booking-First
- Restaurant, café, bakery: Menu-First
- Multi-location chain: Multi-Location
- Law firm, accountant: Proximity-Action (with credential emphasis)
