# Local Business: Audience Psychology

Domain knowledge for local business sites.
Part of: references/domains/local-business/

## Proximity-Based Intent Model

Local business visitors are NOT on an awareness journey. They already know they need the service. The relevant model is proximity-based intent:

| Visitor Type | Mental State | Primary Need | Conversion Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Urgent need** | "My pipe is leaking right now" | Phone number immediately | Click-to-call, "Open Now" status |
| **Scheduled need** | "I need a dentist next month" | Comparing 2-3 options | Reviews, credentials, convenience |
| **Research/comparison** | "Which gym in my area is best?" | Comparing amenities, pricing, reviews | Best reviews + clearest information |
| **Returning customer** | "What are the hours?" | Logistics — phone, hours, address | Quick-find information architecture |

### Urgent Need Visitor
Zero browsing tolerance. Arrived via "near me" search. Needs phone number visible immediately — 76% of "near me" searchers visit a business within 24 hours. Every second of delay costs this conversion.

### Scheduled Need Visitor
Has time to compare. Opens 2-3 local business websites in tabs. Scans for: reviews, credentials, pricing signals, convenience (location, hours, online booking). The winner is usually "best reviews + easiest to book."

### Research/Comparison Visitor
Evaluating the category, not just one provider. Comparing across Google Maps results. Needs: clear service descriptions, transparent pricing (or "free estimate" CTA), strong review presence, competitive differentiators.

### Returning Customer
Already chose this business. Coming back for logistics: hours, phone number, menu, booking link. Needs: information findable in <5 seconds. If they can't find hours quickly, they'll Google it instead of using the site.

## Conversion Triggers

| Trigger | Mechanism | Where it appears |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Google reviews | 84% trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations | Trust bar, review widget |
| Proximity confirmation | "0.8 miles away" / service area match | Hero, service area map |
| "Open Now" status | Immediate availability confirmation | Header, hours block |
| Credentials display | Licenses, certifications, years in business | Trust bar, about section |
| Before/after photos | Visual proof of work quality | Social proof section, service pages |
| Free estimate CTA | Removes pricing anxiety without commitment | Hero, service pages |

## "Coming from X" — Cross-Domain Warnings

| Coming from... | Watch for... |
|----------------|--------------|
| **B2B SaaS** | Over-complicating the message and navigation. Local business visitors need phone, hours, and services — not feature tours, pricing tables, or "How It Works" explainers. Kill the sales funnel thinking. |
| **E-commerce** | Adding unnecessary product-catalog UX. Local businesses don't need filters, sort controls, or product grids. Services are 3-6 items, not 500 SKUs. |
| **Portfolio** | Making it about the brand story instead of logistics. Local customers don't care about the founder's journey — they care about "are you open, are you nearby, are you good." |
| **Media / Content** | Building a content-heavy blog strategy. Local SEO matters, but the site's job is to convert searchers into callers/bookers, not to create a publishing platform. |
| **Nonprofit** | Emphasizing mission over service delivery. Customers hire plumbers because pipes leak, not because they believe in the plumber's values. Lead with competence and availability. |
