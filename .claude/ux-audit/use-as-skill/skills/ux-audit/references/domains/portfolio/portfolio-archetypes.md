# Portfolio: Strategic Archetypes

Domain knowledge for portfolio / personal brand sites.
Part of: references/domains/portfolio/

---

Archetypes define what a site optimizes for at the architectural level — how pages connect, what narrative the site tells, and which user journey is prioritized.

**These are SITE-LEVEL archetypes** (what the overall site structure optimizes for). They are distinct from PAGE-LEVEL layout patterns in `patterns.md`, which define the internal structure of individual pages.

---

## Credibility-Led (Solo Practitioner)
**Optimizes for:** Qualified inquiry rate (contact form submissions or booked discovery calls from ideal-fit clients).
**Best fit:** Freelance designers, developers, copywriters, photographers, solo consultants, coaches, independent creatives — anyone selling personal expertise as a service.
**Sacrifices:** Volume. Deliberately filters out non-ideal clients. Prioritizes quality of inquiry over quantity.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with identity statement ("I help [specific audience] achieve [specific outcome]") + personal photo — not a company logo
- Navigation: Minimal, 4-5 items max: Work, About, Services, Contact. No dropdowns. No "Resources" section unless actively publishing
- Key pages: Homepage (often single-page), Case Studies/Work, About, Services (optional), Contact
- Social proof: Client testimonials with name + company + photo, client logos, specific outcome metrics ("Increased conversion rate by 34%")

---

## Showcase-Led (Creative Portfolio)
**Optimizes for:** Visual impact and aesthetic judgment — the work speaks first, context comes second.
**Best fit:** Photographers, illustrators, graphic designers, art directors, motion designers — creatives where visual quality IS the qualification. Clients hire based on style match, not outcome metrics.
**Sacrifices:** Process transparency, outcome measurement, written case studies. The work is evaluated on aesthetics, not ROI.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with full-bleed imagery or project grid — minimal text, visual-first
- Navigation: Ultra-minimal — Work, About, Contact. Often hidden or hamburger-only
- Key pages: Homepage (gallery), Project pages (image-heavy with minimal text), About, Contact
- Social proof: Awards (Awwwards, Behance features), publication features, client brand recognition

---

## Thought-Leader (Expert Personal Brand)
**Optimizes for:** Authority positioning that generates inbound opportunities (speaking invitations, consulting inquiries, book/course sales, podcast invitations).
**Best fit:** Consultants, coaches, authors, speakers, industry experts — anyone whose reputation IS the product. The site sells the person's thinking, not their execution.
**Sacrifices:** Portfolio depth. Work samples take a back seat to ideas, writing, speaking, and media presence.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with person's name, title/tagline, and credibility markers (book cover, "As seen in" logos, keynote photo)
- Navigation: About, Speaking, Writing/Blog, Consulting, Contact
- Key pages: Homepage, About (detailed bio), Speaking (topics + past events), Blog/Newsletter, Book/Course page, Contact/Hire
- Social proof: Media appearances, speaking engagements, book sales, subscriber count, "As featured in" logos

---

## Agency-Hybrid (Small Studio)
**Optimizes for:** Balance between personal credibility and team capability — "you get me AND a team."
**Best fit:** 2-5 person studios, design partnerships, freelancers who subcontract — any operation larger than solo but smaller than a full agency.
**Sacrifices:** The intimacy of a solo practitioner and the scale impression of a large agency. Must thread the needle.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage opens with studio identity + team value prop ("A small team that punches above its weight")
- Navigation: Work, Services, Team, Blog (optional), Contact
- Key pages: Homepage, Case Studies, Services, Team, Contact
- Social proof: Client logos, case studies with team credits, client testimonials mentioning the team dynamic

## Agency-Studio-Led (Enterprise Branding Agency)
**Optimizes for:** New-business inquiry quality from CMO/Marketing Director buyers — not volume, not SEO, not broad accessibility.
**Best fit:** Branding agencies, creative studios, brand identity firms, motion/kinetic identity studios, naming and verbal identity practices, design consultancies pitching to enterprise clients. Pentagram, Collins, Wolff Olins, DIA Studio, Moving Brands model.
**Sacrifices:** Volume, broad accessibility, SEO-first content strategy, conversion rate optimization. Quantity of inquiries is irrelevant — quality and fit are everything.

**Site structure signal:**
- Homepage: full-bleed motion or hero image + minimal copy; agency name or single tagline only; no hero paragraph explaining what the agency does
- Navigation: minimal (Work, About, Process / How We Work, Contact); no dropdowns; no Resources, Blog, or Case Studies in nav
- Case study grid: curated 6–12 projects (not 50+); organized by client sector + service type; filter bar with industry tags (tech, consumer, healthcare, cultural institutions) and service tags (brand identity, naming, motion identity, packaging); default sort: strategic relevance, not chronological
- Team and leadership section: prominent senior profiles; partner/principal bios with work attribution
- Thought leadership / POV: optional; signals cultural depth and strategic positioning; not a blog

**Conversion path:**
Recommendation → website visit → 2–3 targeted case studies → Process / Approach page → team bios → Contact (inquiry form). Average lead cycle: 1.5 years. 79% of CMO buyers arrive via recommendation — the site validates the recommendation, it does not generate the lead.

**"Website as portfolio piece" quality constraint:**
The site IS evaluated as a design artifact. Every layout decision is read as a capability signal. Standards:
* Whitespace: 15–20% page margins minimum; generous line height
* Typography: maximum 2 typeface families; type scales with clear hierarchy
* Motion: scroll-triggered reveals (not decorative looping); branded page transitions between case studies; custom cursors only in deliberate engagement zones (not sitewide)
* Quality bar: Awwwards 4-dimension standard — Design 40%, Usability 30%, Creativity 20%, Content 10%

**CMO buyer evaluation lens:**
CMOs judge site design quality as a direct proxy for creative capability: "If their own site looks like this, imagine what they'd do for us." They evaluate:
* Creative taste and design quality
* Strategic rationale in case studies (not just beautiful outcomes)
* Process transparency ("Do they have a repeatable methodology?")
* Cultural alignment ("Would I enjoy working with them?")
* Relevant sector experience ("Have they done this for someone like me?")

**CMO vs. CEO evaluation difference:**
* CMO: Judges design quality directly; evaluates creative-strategic integration; 76% say creativity must link to long-term brand strategy; wants to see the thinking, not just the output
* CEO: Focuses on business outcomes and credentials; less sensitive to design quality of the site itself; more interested in ROI metrics and client logos

**Anti-signals (will end the consideration):**
* Generic or template-based site design
* No strategic rationale in case studies (beautiful outputs without "why")
* Process completely invisible
* No senior profiles or leadership bios
* All work looks identical (no range or creative voice)

## Archetype Selection

When choosing an archetype:

1. **What sells the work** — Visual quality? Measured outcomes? Ideas and reputation?
2. **Team size** — Solo? Creative partner? Small studio?
3. **Client sophistication** — Are clients hiring for aesthetics, strategy, or thought leadership?

**Default combinations:**
- Solo freelancer (strategy/consulting): Credibility-Led
- Solo creative (design/photo/illustration): Showcase-Led
- Author/speaker/coach: Thought-Leader
- Small design studio: Agency-Hybrid
- Branding agency or creative studio (enterprise clients): Agency-Studio-Led
